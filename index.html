Every election happening in 2027

A complete calendar of federal, statewide, legislative and judicial races. 2027 is an off-year — the entire federal ballot is empty, and the real action is three governorships and eight legislative chambers.

There are no regular federal elections in 2027

This is the single most important fact about the year, so it sits at the top rather than buried in a table:

    The only federal races that can occur in 2027 are special elections to fill vacancies. As of July 2026 none are scheduled, and no current vacancy carries into 2027 — all five outstanding U.S. House vacancies resolve during 2026. Historically a Congress sees roughly 5–10 House specials across its two years, so expect a handful, but naming any of them today would be guesswork. Re-check in Q1 2027.

    How to read this page

    • General election dates are firm. They are fixed by statute and, for Louisiana, by the Secretary of State's published 2027 calendar.
    • Primary dates marked “projected” are not. No state has published an official 2027 election calendar yet, so those dates are derived from statutory formulas. Virginia already moved its 2026 primary by statute once — treat June 2027 as a default, not a lock.
    • Louisiana votes on Saturdays, and every 2027 date was advanced one week. The general is Nov 20, not Nov 13 — confirmed against the Secretary of State's published 2027 calendar. The shift happens because R.S. 18:402 bars elections on Sukkot (Oct 16, 2027) and on the three days after Thanksgiving (Nov 26–28, 2027). Wikipedia currently shows Nov 13, applying the base formula without those exclusions — if you cross-check this page against it, that is the discrepancy you will find.
    • Louisiana also holds two earlier statewide election days: April 17, 2027 (BESE party primary and municipal primary — the date carrying the SB 68 ballot measure) and May 29, 2027 (municipal general).

    Known gaps — stated plainly

    • The local section is curated, not exhaustive. It covers big-city mayoralties and the local races with genuine national salience. It does not attempt the full county sweep: Pennsylvania (all 67 counties), Mississippi (all 82) and Virginia each elect a complete slate of county offices in 2027, running to thousands of races. Much of that data could only be inferred from term-length arithmetic rather than sourced, so it is left out rather than guessed at.
    • City council seats are noted on the mayoral entries, not listed individually. Chicago (50 wards), Philadelphia (17), Houston (16), Nashville (40), Indianapolis (25), Denver (13) and Jacksonville (19) all elect their full councils in 2027.
    • School boards are largely excluded. The one firm finding worth recording: there is no Chicago Board of Education election in 2027 — Illinois runs those in even years, and the January 2027 seating of the fully elected board is an inauguration, not an election.
    • Ballot measures are near-empty by nature, not by oversight. Only one measure is certified nationwide. Texas is normally the largest source of off-year propositions, but its legislature does not convene until January 2027, so no Texas measure can exist yet. Maine and Wisconsin have pending items that are not certified.
    • Ballotpedia blocked automated access throughout this research. Figures come from Wikipedia, state statute text, secretary of state calendars and local news instead. Search snippets returned stale data at least once, so rows that could not be independently cross-checked deserve more skepticism.
